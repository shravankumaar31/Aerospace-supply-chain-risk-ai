# RISK BRIEF: NAICS 336411 — Aircraft Manufacturing

## Executive Summary

Aircraft Manufacturing exhibits Medium composite risk (46.43/100) driven primarily by trade concentration: 60% of $10.0B in exports flow to a single destination country. Despite moderate workforce challenges (32.44/100), the segment's export dependency on one trading partner creates material procurement vulnerability.

## Risk Drivers

• **Export destination concentration** — 60.42/100 risk score indicates approximately 60% of the segment's $10.0 billion annual exports depend on a single foreign customer nation, exposing U.S. prime contractors to demand shocks from policy shifts, currency volatility, or geopolitical friction in that market.

• **Workforce availability pressure** — with 240,989 employed workers and a 32.44/100 workforce risk score, the segment faces moderate but persistent talent pipeline constraints, particularly for specialized roles in composite fabrication, avionics integration, and FAA-certified assembly.

• **Scale-dependent resilience** — $10.0B export base and nearly 241,000 workers suggest deep industrial capacity, yet the Medium composite score (46.43/100) signals that size alone does not mitigate concentration risks when trade flows lack geographic diversification.

## Market Context

Aircraft Manufacturing forms the apex of the U.S. aerospace industrial base, anchoring complex multi-tier supply chains across propulsion, structures, and avionics. The segment's $10.0B export portfolio underscores its role as a critical balance-of-trade contributor, but the 60% single-destination share reveals structural exposure uncommon in diversified defense portfolios. This trade dependency likely reflects large commercial aircraft sales to a high-volume buyer (e.g., China or a Middle Eastern hub), creating program-level vulnerabilities if that relationship sours. Workforce fundamentals remain serviceable but not immune to skilled labor shortages plaguing advanced manufacturing nationwide.

## Recommended Actions

• **Procurement Directors**: Require Tier-1 aircraft OEMs to disclose end-customer concentration by platform in annual supplier business reviews; flag programs where >40% of revenue derives from the single export destination identified in this analysis.

• **Program Managers**: Diversify platform export campaigns to reduce single-country dependencies; prioritize customer development in allied markets (Europe, Indo-Pacific partners) where regulatory harmonization enables faster certification.

• **Supply Chain Risk Officers**: Model scenario impacts of a 25% demand reduction from the primary export destination; pre-qualify alternate suppliers or domestic absorption capacity to buffer workforce layoffs and preserve critical skills.
