# Supply Chain Risk Brief: California Aerospace Supplier Base

## Executive Summary

California's aerospace supplier base presents a **Low** overall risk profile (composite score: 16.82/100) despite commanding $125.4 billion in contract value across 1,396 recipients. The state's moderate market concentration (HHI: 1,682) balances large prime presence against sufficient competitive depth to mitigate single-source vulnerabilities.

## Risk Drivers

- **Scale concentration without market lock-in**: California holds $125.4 billion in aerospace contracts—the largest state allocation—yet maintains an HHI of 1,682, indicating no dominant monopolistic control despite the presence of major defense primes and commercial aerospace manufacturers.

- **Broad supplier distribution**: With 1,396 distinct contract recipients, the state demonstrates meaningful industrial diversity that reduces exposure to individual supplier failures or capacity constraints.

- **Moderate concentration flag**: The "Moderate" concentration risk label signals pockets of dependency within specific subsectors or commodities, warranting subsegment monitoring even as aggregate risk remains contained.

## Market Context

California anchors the U.S. aerospace industrial base with unmatched scale and breadth. The state's ecosystem spans commercial aviation (Southern California airframe integration), defense primes (missiles, space systems), and deep semiconductor/avionics supply chains concentrated in Silicon Valley corridors. While the 1,396-recipient count suggests distributed capacity, California's dominance in specialized niches—precision manufacturing, composite structures, mission software—creates hidden single-points-of-failure not captured in state-level aggregates. Regional workforce pipelines, particularly around Los Angeles and the Bay Area, remain critical to sustaining this capacity, though data on labor availability is not provided in this analysis.

## Recommended Actions

- **Map subsector dependencies**: Conduct NAICS-level drill-down within California's supplier base to identify commodities where concentration exceeds the state's 1,682 aggregate HHI, prioritizing sole-source or dual-source components.

- **Monitor seismic and wildfire exposure**: Assess which of the 1,396 recipients operate in high-risk zones for natural disasters; develop continuity protocols for suppliers holding critical single-source positions.

- **Benchmark against alternate states**: Compare California's per-recipient contract value and HHI against Texas, Washington, and Arizona aerospace corridors to evaluate strategic rebalancing opportunities without sacrificing technical capability.
